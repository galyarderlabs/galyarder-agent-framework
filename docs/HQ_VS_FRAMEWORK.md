# Galyarder HQ vs. Galyarder Framework

## TL;DR

**Galyarder Framework (Intelligence)**: The repository you are in. It contains the 40 specialized agents and 132 skills that perform the actual work. It works standalone in your IDE or CLI.

**Galyarder HQ (Control Plane)**: A separate repository ([galyarder-hq](https://github.com/galyarderlabs/galyarder-hq)) that provides a web-based dashboard to monitor, govern, and orchestrate multiple Galyarder projects from one place.

---

## Galyarder Framework: The Intelligence Layer

### What It Is
The "brain" of your digital company. It consists of high-integrity protocols (Humans 3.0) and a workforce of autonomous agents.

### How It Works
- You chat with an autonomous assistant (Claude Code, Cursor, Gemini).
- The Framework provides the **Logic** and **SOPs**.
- Agents execute tasks using the **1-Man Army Protocols**.

### Use This If:
- You are a solo founder building a specific product.
- You want your AI to follow strict engineering and marketing standards.
- You prefer working directly in your terminal or IDE.

---

## Galyarder HQ: The Control Plane

### What It Is
The "office" of your digital enterprise. It is a web platform for managing the lifecycle of multiple agent-driven companies.

### How It Works
- **Discovery**: HQ automatically indexes any project initialized with `galyarder-scaffold`.
- **Governance**: A master view of all Linear tickets, Obsidian reports, and agent activity.
- **Orchestration**: A central hub to trigger "missions" across different repositories.

### Use This If:
- You are managing multiple startups or projects simultaneously.
- You want a visual overview of your company's progress and history.
- You need centralized governance and reporting.

---

## The Relationship: The Brain and the Dashboard

```text
Framework = The Employees (SOPs, Agents, Skills)
HQ        = The Headquarters (UI, Monitoring, Governance)

Framework = The Intelligence that builds the code.
HQ        = The Interface that manages the business.
```

**The Framework works perfectly standalone.** You do not need Galyarder HQ to build institutional-grade products, but HQ provides the asymmetrical leverage needed to scale a 1-Man Army into a 1-Man Empire.
